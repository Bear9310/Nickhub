Understanding Crypto Markets

What is Market Cap?

Market capitalization equals current price multiplied by the circulating supply. It's a more reliable measure of a cryptocurrency's relative size than price alone. Bitcoin's market cap exceeds $1 trillion, representing roughly 50% of the total crypto market.

What Drives Crypto Prices?

Cryptocurrency prices are influenced by supply and demand dynamics, regulatory news, macroeconomic conditions, technological developments, social sentiment, and institutional adoption. Unlike stocks, crypto markets operate 24/7 with no circuit breakers, making volatility much higher.

How to Read Live Data

The 24h change percentage shows how much a coin's price moved in the last day. Green numbers indicate gains; red indicates losses. Trading volume shows how much of a coin was bought/sold — high volume during a price move signals stronger conviction in that direction.

What Is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a decentralized database shared across thousands of computers simultaneously. Unlike traditional databases controlled by a single company or bank, a blockchain is maintained by a global network where no single party has control.

Each "block" contains transaction data, a timestamp, and a cryptographic hash of the previous block — chaining them together so that historical records cannot be altered without redoing all subsequent computational work. This makes blockchain data tamper-resistant by design.

Bitcoin's blockchain is the oldest and most battle-tested, running continuously for over 15 years without a successful hack of the core protocol. Ethereum extended this concept with programmable smart contracts — code that runs automatically when conditions are met, enabling DeFi, NFTs, and DAOs.

Why does blockchain matter? It enables trustless systems — two parties who don't know or trust each other can transact directly, with the blockchain acting as an impartial, mathematically-verifiable record keeper. No bank, no lawyer, no middleman required.

Different blockchains make different tradeoffs. Bitcoin prioritizes security and decentralization over speed. Solana prioritizes speed (65,000+ TPS) over decentralization. BNB Chain balances speed and cost with some centralization. There is no perfect blockchain — only the right tool for each use case.
